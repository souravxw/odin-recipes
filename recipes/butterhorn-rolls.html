Butterhorn Roll

Decription

Butterhorn dinner rolls date all the way back to colonial times

the process to make this recipe easier for new and inexperienced bakers

These classic dinner rolls are light but rich, and slightly sweet

They're perfect for special occasions or holiday dinners, alongside any main course.

Ingradients

  • 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
  • ¼ cup warm water
  • 1 cup milk
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • 3 ¾ cups all-purpose flour, divided
  • 1 ½ teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • ¼ cup butter, melted

Steps

  1. Sprinkle yeast onto warm water. Stir gently and let sit until foamy and frothy, 15 to 20 minutes.
  2. Transfer dough to a floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic, about 5 minutes.
  3. Cover and let rise until almost doubled in size, about 1 hour
  4. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Line a baking sheet with a silicone liner (such as Silpat®).
  5. Let rolls sit until they puff up a little, 15 to 20 minutes.
